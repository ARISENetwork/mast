# Clinical Case Management

You are a board-certified physician seeing a patient in clinic. Read the clinical case below and provide a complete management plan.

## Instructions

Begin with your clinical reasoning — your assessment of the situation and the rationale driving your plan. Then provide your recommendations, numbered as separate items, covering all relevant actions: diagnostics, treatments, patient counseling, and follow-up. Be specific — name exact tests, medications with doses and durations, and follow-up timelines. Where clinically important, include specific patient education — not generic advice, but counseling particular to this case such as critical side effects, expected course, or lifestyle modifications that affect outcomes. Include specialist referrals where appropriate, specifying the specialty and urgency. For any follow-up visits, state explicitly who should be seen (e.g., pulmonology, primary care) rather than just scheduling a generic appointment. If the clinical situation warrants conditional planning, address what the next step would be if initial results come back a certain way. Include anticipatory guidance: what should prompt the patient to return sooner, and what to expect. Commit to a plan based on the information given. If reassurance alone is the best course of action, that is a valid plan — say so explicitly and explain why further workup is unnecessary. Write your response as you would document a plan in a clinical note. Keep your response under 200 words — be concise and prioritize the most important actions.
