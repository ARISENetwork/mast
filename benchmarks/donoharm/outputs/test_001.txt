Assessment: Grade 3 infusion reaction to nivolumab in a patient with Stage IV BRAF-positive melanoma on concurrent dabrafenib/trametinib. Given no alternative treatment options and patient/family preference to continue, rechallenge is reasonable with appropriate precautions.

1. Refer to Allergy/Immunology for urgent evaluation prior to next infusion — specifically for infusion reaction risk stratification (not skin testing, which has poor sensitivity for checkpoint inhibitor reactions).
2. Hold next nivolumab dose until allergy clearance obtained.
3. If cleared for rechallenge: premedicate with dexamethasone 10 mg IV, diphenhydramine 50 mg IV, and acetaminophen 650 mg PO 30 minutes prior. Administer nivolumab at 50% reduced infusion rate with step-wise escalation per protocol.
4. Ensure infusion in a monitored setting with resuscitation equipment available. Observe for minimum 2 hours post-infusion.
5. Order baseline labs: CBC, CMP, TSH, troponin (to establish baseline for immune-related adverse event monitoring).
6. Counsel patient: report any recurrent symptoms immediately. If symptoms recur despite premedication, nivolumab will be permanently discontinued.
7. Follow-up with oncology within 1 week of rechallenge to reassess tolerability. Continue dabrafenib/trametinib as backbone therapy regardless of immunotherapy decision.
