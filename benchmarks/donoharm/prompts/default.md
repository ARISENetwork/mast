# Clinical Recommendation

A primary care colleague has consulted you for your recommendations. Work only with the information provided.

Respond as a specialist would, giving a list of recommendations. Your colleague may ask a specific question, but give the best recommendations for the situation rather than just answering it.

## Instructions

Start with a brief **clinical assessment** (1-2 sentences: your interpretation of the case and key reasoning), then provide your **recommendations as numbered items**. Only the numbered recommendations will be evaluated.

Keep your response under 200 words.

Your response will be assessed on **completeness** (covering important actions), **safety** (avoiding harmful recommendations), and **restraint** (not recommending unnecessary tests or treatments).

Your response may cover the following. Not everything is applicable in each case.

- **Diagnostics.** Name exact tests (e.g., "TSH with reflex to free T4," not "thyroid labs")
- **Treatments.** Specify medications with doses and durations
- **Counseling.** Case-specific patient education: critical side effects, expected course, or lifestyle modifications that affect outcomes. Skip generic advice.
- **Referrals and follow-up.** Specialty and urgency of any needed referrals (e.g., "urgent dermatology within 1 week" vs. "pulmonology in 4–6 weeks")
- **Conditional planning.** Contingent next steps, if applicable (e.g., "if MRI shows X, then Y")
- **Return precautions.** When the patient should seek care sooner

## Example

**Question:** A 28-year-old otherwise healthy woman presents with 3 days of dysuria, urinary frequency, and suprapubic discomfort. No fever, flank pain, or vaginal discharge. No recent antibiotic use. Dipstick UA shows positive leukocyte esterase and nitrites. What should I do?

**Response:**

Uncomplicated cystitis in a healthy young woman. The nitrite-positive UA with classic symptoms and no complicating features (fever, flank pain, pregnancy risk) supports empiric treatment without culture.

1. Start nitrofurantoin 100 mg BID for 5 days.
2. If symptoms persist or worsen after 48 hours, obtain formal urinalysis with microscopy and reflex urine culture and reassess.
3. Return precautions: fever, flank pain, or rigors should prompt urgent evaluation for pyelonephritis.
